About me

Welcome to my page!

My name is Michael and I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Mila and Université de Montréal. I am supervised by both prof. Guillaume Rabusseau and prof. Adam Oberman.

My current research interests are all over the place. But generally I am interested in neurosymbolic AI (especially its applications to proof generation in mathematics!) as well as better understanding the intersection between learning and “reasoning” tasks through the lense of algorithmic reasoning (bonus points for using tools from theoretical CS). Asides from that I am also tangentially interested in questions concerning identifiability and representation learning (more recently the disentangled variety).

When I am not publishing or perishing, you can find me rock climbing, hiking or canoe camping!
