Michael Rizvi-Martel

Hi! I'm Michael, a PhD student in Computer Science at Mila and Université de Montréal. I am co-supervised by Guillaume Rabusseau (UdeM & Mila) and Adam Oberman (McGill & LawZero). I am also fortunate to be mentored by Michael Hahn.

My research interests are all over the place, but I am generally interested in better understanding the limitations and capacities of modern AI systems. My work is guided by questions such as:

  • Why do LLMs sometimes fail on tasks that appear simple yet excel on certain tasks difficult for humans?
  • What fundamental principles govern learning in these models?

So far, this has led me to a blend of interpretability and theoretical research. I strongly believe that understanding AI systems more deeply can help us make them safer, anticipate future technological developments, and design better-informed policies.
